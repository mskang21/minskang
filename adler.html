DURATION:Summer 2022

COLLABORATORS:Naryeong Kim, Seongjun Jo

In the summer of 2022, I helped a a VR start up called Adler based in Seoul. The startup was on its way to launching a beta version service of its initial 3D vr gallery where users could easily create a virtual gallery with their photos uploaded in a 3D space. With the service, the company able to raise about $2 million at a valuation of $6 million through venture capitalists' fundings. The newly designed product aimed to increase the user interaction as well as easier, faster, and aesthetically pleasing services. I was able to learn more about designing user experience for a SNs platform while contemplating the implementations of novel technical aspects of the service such as three.js.

Organizing Research

User Research

Visual Artists

I interviewed artists around the age early 20s and 30s to find the user need to create spaces in Adler. These artists wanted more user interactsions with other creators in the Space to find partnership opportunities with visitors.

Musicians

These users were musicians from the previous MVP of the product where they were only able to create simple 3D galleries with limited upload functionalities. They faced the need to find functions to upload music they make to the space which was not implemented previously due to loading speed issues.

Non-Artists

These users were non-creators who utitlized the MVP of our service to make 3D gallery spaces for their family photos and other documented photos that commemorate the memories of special events. These users wanted more functionalities that enable them to decorate the space to their taste without efforts of creating decorations by themselves.

From these Interviews, we were able to distinguish what improvements should be made to make a more easily accessible, customizable, Interactive and less time consuming upload process.

Market Analysis

Competitive Research

For Market Research we decided to look into other 3D paltofrms among which there were companies that provide 3D models at purchase, provide 3D vr experience gears, provide services to create different spaces depending on users' customization, and etc. The common porblems among these companies was a lack of fast and easily accesible vr experience without compatibility issues with devices and equipments. There were also common problems with many users ot able to reach the minimum level of understanding the method of using these devices and services for an active user engagement within the service.

Insights

Main Insights from Interviews

After rounds of interviews where my team asked questions above with following up questions that varied in each interview process, we were able to discover main insights about the underlying problems and the painpoints of current manga enthusiasts that actively use existing mobile and desktop platforms to view mangas

1. Users want to change the space easily while and after uploading contents.

2. Users want more functionalities with faster loading time of the gallery.

3. Users would like to communicate with eachother online as if they were talking in real life.

4. Users want to access the spaces they create anywhere with no limitiation of devices they use.

Defining Scope

Narrowing the Problem Space

After gaining The main insights that explain the pain ponits of the users, we devided to broaden out scope into investigating what problems are really causing these issues and needs so that out team would be able to develop how can we questions with a wider view into assessing the problem space. As a result, the team concluded that the above problems shown by the insights originated from the problems below.

1. A lot of lucrative manga contents do not reach western market

2. Lack of platforms that provide accessible and stable manga reading experience.

Synthesizing 1)

Stakeholder Mapping

We moved onto making stakeholder mapping to cateogirze users in levels of high usage and low usage in terms of how much they would utilize our service. The primary stakeholders were artists and content creators interested in promoting their works online in an accessible way. the secondary stakeholders were art appreciators who would like to view works of their favorite artists on line. the tertiary stakeholders were marketers doing research on artists to collaborate with.

Synthesizing 2)

Personas

In order to specify users who would use our service, we cateogirzed personas into two different people one of which was a visual artist and and another of which was an online influnecers. By thinking about the personas of these people, we were able to visualize what it would be like to undergo painpoints we figured out from user research process which allowed us to generate better HowCanWe questions.

How Can We Questions

Top Three Questions

With the focuses of these questions we branched off into three main questions that we focued on takling the users' biggest painpoints. For SNIIP, we decided to increase user interactions not only between users but also authors, minimize the effort in selecting favorites from large pool of mangas available online, make the mangas readily available to read, and create a customizable environment where users can create personal spaces for the most optimized user experience.

1. How might we allow users to feel comfortable to speak in the real time when they are in space?

2. How might we allow users to express themselves with a functionality that is faster to access compared to virtual 3D space?

3. How might we allow users to check what genres and categories are available to see what is trending or not?

Reflections

difficulties

Since this was entirely a remote internship, all communications and work sessions were carried out remotely. Also, with teammates from different parts of hte country and the world, we had to coordinate our time to find the time where we could meet despite differences in our time zones. It was difficult to intuitively communicate with the teammates when we were attempting to make changes in our initial UX decisions. We overcame this problem by actively using communication Saas tool and holding weekly meeting for the design and the general team meeting with the developers.
