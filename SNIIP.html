DURATION: Spring 2023 ~ Summer 2023

COLLABORATORS: Gaby Ruiz, Vicky Wang

In the summer of 2023, I created a mobile platform for a Entertainment StartUp SNIIP. The product intended to allow readers to have a more pleasant manga reading experience compared to other competitors in the manga industry. This was a renewed design from a previous desktop platform so that readers could have more accessiblity to our service on their phone. Before launching the product, the company was able to raise $___ before launching the product. The goal of designing the platform was to allow readers in western countries to have more access to different types of mangas that are yet to be exported due to limited streaming or entertainment services and complex legality reasons.

Final Solution

Mobile Manga Platform

A mobile and desktop manga viewing platform with personalized selections of mangas in customizable lists and libraries. The product makes mangas accessible to readers over the world by implementing a stable platform with faster updates of translated versions to provide a larger audience to authors and a pleasant reading experience for users.

Formal Research

Competitive Research

Currently, most platforms face legality issues with distributing mangas to readers without consent from publishers or from authors. These mangas are basically exposed without any loyalties given to the creators of the manga as well as the formal distributors. Also, the platforms tend to crash often, increasing stability issues for the users. The domains of these websites also change frequently to avoid lawful cessation. The users also face problems with random ads popping up each time they try clicking a user interface, making the user experience full of unexpected pauses and exposure to contents.

Primary Research

Semi Structured Interviews, Surveys

For the primary research, Interviews and Surveys were conducted virtually through zoom meetings and google surveys. These methods allowed to research the resources that readers have varying from the platforms they use to how frequent or pleasant their reading experiences are. The results from the research were categorized into Data visualizations such as bar charts and pie charts straight from the surveys. The targets of the research included following:

1. undergraduate university students who read mangas or have read mangas both online and offline

2. Manga publishers

Insights

Main Insights from Interviews

After rounds of interviews where my team asked questions above with following up questions that varied in each interview process, we were able to discover main insights about the underlying problems and the painpoints of current manga enthusiasts that actively use existing mobile and desktop platforms to view mangas

1. Users want to be able to freely communicate with each other when they enter the virtual space

2. users want to upload the pictures without technical restrictions as to sizes of photos

3. Users would like to get instant updates in the notifications to find who liked or followed their spaces or accounts

4. Users want clear categories and genres that they can access better

Defining Scope

Narrowing the Problem Space

After gaining insights from the interviews with YMCA staff, we utilized the insights to narrow down rough problems mentioned during the interviews to smaller ones so that we can be sure what is the underlying painpoints and needs for the youth. After the filtering process, the follwings were the main discoveries that concerned the both the youth and the YMCa branches.

1. Students are not interested or involved in community activities both inside and outside the Y.

2. Youth are not aware of programs that match their interests.

3. Some programs do not have altruistic purposes since such programs focus more on campaigning the issue rather than resolving the issue practically.

How Can We Questions

Affinity Diagraming

From the four main insights, my team decided to brainstorm "How can We" questions in order to determine the what painpoints and needs we will mainly tackle to improve youth's experience in engaging in more YMCA programs or their own community programs they discover or build independently. the follwings are three main questions we attempted to tackle:

1. How can users feel their interactions with the authors and their access to the mangas trustworthy?

2. How might we minimized user effort in searching for mangas?

3. How might we allow users to customize the app according to their preferences of mangas?

Synthesizing 1)

Stakeholder Mapping

For the synthesizing process, we have decided to identify our primary, secondary, and tertiary users of the product. Our primary users included, readers who want a personalized reading experience and the users in western countries who want to understand more mangas.

Synthesizing 2)

Personas

We have come up with two personas whic included: a manga publisher/artist, and an manga enthusiast. We decied the personas to be two types of customers of our product which were authors and readers. During the design process, this persona construction helped to remind ourselves that the product not only aims to attract readers, but also authors and small creators who want more exposure of their products to the public.

Brainstorming: Crazy 8

Possible Ideas

Through a rapid brainsotrming session within the team and the developers, we came up with ideas as to how we were going to tackle our main goal of the product which was to make manga experience Interactive, Accessible, and Personalized. With the following choices below - making a page for the author of a manga to display a simple biography with his or her other works; displaying analytics for how often, what types, what genres, and so on regarding the reader's reading history, building a personal library for users to save works to cotinusouly read, making a gamified design, and attractive thumbnails - our team decided to choose ideas of "Gamified design," "Author introduction space," and a "Personal library" as out main focuses.

Architecture

Functionalities

With these decisions in mind, we have decided to layout functionalities before jumping into designs. My team used a google sheet to layout functionalities for each page with rough description and ideas for their UI compoenents and navigation methods. We have also planned out the animation descriptions intuitively so that each member of the team could focus on the sections of the product he or she is designated with. Despite having a general requirement documented before jumping into prototyping, it was important that we allowed a lot of rooms for changes and intuitive work process rather than a systematic task oritented prototyping.

User Flow

On top of the functionality specifications, the team deicded to build a User flow map with guidance as to which icon leads to which page with various navigation methods such as, on click, on drag, and etc. This allowed us to keep in mind how we would connect the flows between the pages we create so that we can build a working prototype when we finish high fidelity prototypes.

Information Architecture

The team begun building a information architecture for our mobile product so that we could recollect in case we forget what decisions we have made in the process of brainstorming and affinity diagraming.

Design System

Fonts and Colors

For the design system, we tried orange and organelle as the main colors for the design since these colors satisfied our criteria for having a both professional and playful vibe within the platform so that young stakeholders both feel the credibility of our system and feel more familiarity with their possibly first time working independently for the career paths of their interests. We also added geometrical designs that took inspiration from organic curves and shaped to decrease excessive sharpness in the presentation again to focus on the younger audience.

Break Downs

Final Designs

Pick Genres

The onboarding process allows users to pick their favorite genres for the application to autonomously suggest mangas according to users' preferences in home page.

Personal Library

Users can add or delete mangas they select in their libraries. Multiple libraries can be created up to the users' choice of categorizing various mangas.

Discovery and Communication

Users are able to learn more about authors who produced mangas they like from author page that introduces author's other mangas available in the application. Users can leave comments at the end of each episode of a manga to express their views and thorughts on the stories

Browsing

The app exposed users to many mangas in different filterins such as rankings in each genre, autonomously chosen mangas to users' taste, top selection of most viewed and trending mangas on a daily and weekly basis.
